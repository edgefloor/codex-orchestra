---
name: ground-project
description: Consultant procedure for reaching a decision-complete problem/scope/assurance contract with the Operator.
---

1. Capture Operator intent as outcomes, users, success signals, constraints, preferences, and known non-goals. Mark facts, assumptions, recommendations, and decisions separately.
2. Inspect current repository/product evidence before asking questions. When one bounded domain question requires expertise, run a small read-only Explorer/Advisor branch under a grounding permit and synthesize it yourself.
3. Ask only questions whose answers materially change externally observable behavior, scope boundaries, acceptance, assurance, budget, or irreversible choices. Batch them into a decision-ready set.
4. For reversible ambiguity, record a provisional default, rationale, owner, and invalidation condition; proceed. Do not manufacture blockers from ordinary uncertainty.
5. Produce `BRIEF.md`, `SCOPE.md`, acceptance behavior/examples, decision queue, and `ASSURANCE.yaml`. Derive assurance dimensions separately from maturity labels.
6. Check that every in-scope outcome has acceptance behavior, every boundary/non-goal is explicit, and a Delivery Architect can plan without inventing product requirements.
7. Return one grounding result or one material decision request. Keep expert transcripts and research detail in referenced artifacts.

**Complete when:** Operator and Consultant share a revisioned, testable understanding and remaining unknowns have owners/defaults rather than vague obstacles.
