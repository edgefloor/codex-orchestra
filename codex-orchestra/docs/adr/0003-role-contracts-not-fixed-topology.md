# Treat governance roles as contracts, not a fixed organization chart

Orchestra preserves distinct authority contracts: the Operator accepts material decisions; the Consultant owns Grounding; delivery leadership owns tactical planning, delegation, integration, and first-line review; Reviewers and Verifiers remain independent of implementation. These contracts do not require the legacy fourteen-role, depth-three physical tree in every Engagement.

Custom agent TOMLs define recurring, selectable authority and behavior. Task-specific expertise belongs in a Context Capsule or temporary role instruction rather than a new persistent TOML. Each Engagement forms the smallest capable topology, and a role becomes persistent only when repeated use and evaluation justify it.

## Consequences

- The initial package keeps Consultant, Team Leader, Worker, Reviewer, and Verifier roles.
- Manager, Delivery Architect, Context Engineer, Role Architect, Quality Governor, and specialist roles remain patterns that may be introduced when their separation adds measurable value.
- Physical parentage does not itself grant product, scope, risk, or acceptance authority.
